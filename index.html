The impact

Why belonging keeps students enrolled.

The first few weeks of university matter. Students are finding their way, working out where they fit, and deciding whether this new place feels like somewhere they can stay.

For many students, that decision comes down to the people they meet early: a familiar face in a lecture, a study group that becomes friends, someone who saves them a seat, or the sense that someone would notice if they did not show up.

That kind of connection can change the whole first year. A student who finds people early has more reasons to come back. One who doesn't can end up managing the transition alone, and may quietly drift away before they ever settle in.

What the research shows

In Australia, the national Student Experience Survey found that 18.3% of undergraduates seriously considered leaving. In New Zealand, belonging is recognised as a key driver of retention and completion, especially for Māori and Pacific learners. In Singapore, where graduation rates are high, the challenge often shows up less as dropout and more as first-year stress, pressure, and isolation.

Across these settings, students struggle less because of the coursework and more because of pressure, loneliness, and not fitting in — especially those away from home for the first time.

What Playfair changes

When Playfair's impact has been measured, the results are strong. An analysis of U.S. Department of Education IPEDS data across 32 North American universities found first-year retention rose by 1% to 11% in the year after Playfair was introduced.

In budget terms, that's roughly US$121,000 at smaller campuses to more than US$2.6 million at larger ones. These are North American figures, but the point holds here too: early connection isn't just good for student experience — it can affect whether students stay and thrive.

Why Playfair works

Playfair works because connection is designed, not left to chance. Students take part in dozens of small, low-stakes interactions in a single session.

The activities are active, inclusive, and non-competitive, so everyone can take part: outgoing or introverted, international, first-in-family, or still unsure where they fit. Senior students are trained as peer leaders, so newcomers see a campus where people look out for each other.

That's why universities use Playfair as more than a welcome activity. It is a practical way to build belonging early, strengthen peer connection, and help students start university with more confidence, and more reasons to come back.

The main event · On campus

Ninety minutes. A hall full of strangers. Connected by the end — to each other and to the place.

This is the heart of what we do. Playfair gets students moving, laughing, talking, and meeting people they'd never have met on their own — pulling everyone in quickly, so connection starts right away.

In ninety minutes, most students walk away with more than twenty new connections — people they'll recognise in lectures, dining halls, and group chats all year.

What it actually looks like

Warm, high-energy, and impossible to sit out of.

Playfair brings students together through audience participation, non-competitive games, and team-building — all designed to build school spirit, wellbeing, and a real sense of belonging. It's great fun, but it's not just entertainment. As students play, the facilitator weaves in wellbeing, multicultural awareness, and community support.

The activities are simple and surprising. Students race in threes to find everything they have in common, jumping up to shout out each match. Pairs invent a secret handshake to swap the next time they cross paths. Small groups trade the stories behind their names and the moments that shaped them. Bit by bit, strangers become familiar faces.

Playfair is also leadership training. At every event, at least twenty senior students are trained to lead alongside the facilitator. On campuses that bring Playfair back year after year, those leaders are often students who first experienced it themselves — now creating the same welcome for someone new.

Playfair for conferences & teams

75% of conference attendees say networking is their main reason for attending. Most events still leave that to chance.

People come to conferences, offsites, and team days hoping to meet others, have real conversations, and feel part of something bigger than their own role or organisation. But too often, connection is left to a name badge, a coffee queue, or a few minutes of awkward networking between sessions. Playfair makes those connections happen on purpose.

What it is

Connection built into the room.

Playfair is a live, facilitator-led team-building experience that uses comedy, audience participation, and non-competitive interaction to connect a whole room at once — from a ten-person offsite to a conference floor of a thousand.

Playfair gets people out of receive-mode and into the room with each other. In one session, people who arrived not knowing each other end up having met, shared a laugh, and found a reason to keep talking through every break that follows. It is structured enough to feel safe, active enough to shift the energy, and human enough to make connection feel natural.

Why it works

The opening session sets the tone.

Begin with a keynote and people stay in listening mode. Start with Playfair and they are switched on and connected to the room before the programming begins.

Instead of awkward small talk, Playfair creates dozens of low-stakes introductions in minutes. People meet through shared stories, playful moments, and simple activities that make it easier to keep talking later.

That warmth carries into the rest of the programme. Keynotes land better, breakouts go deeper, and the hallway conversations are rich from day one.

Where it fits

One format, many moments.

Playfair works with ten people or a thousand — a 15-minute reset between sessions, a 90-minute opener, or a full-day experience. It can be light and energising, or focused on trust, leadership, and team connection.

Conference openers

Set the tone before the first keynote and help delegates connect from the start.

Team days & offsites

Build trust and shared learning across departments, regions and levels.

Graduate & new-hire inductions

Help new starters feel part of the team from day one.

Leadership programmes & retreats

Create the conditions for openness, reflection, and real participation.

Led by practitioners

Every activity has a purpose.

Sessions are led by facilitators who are also experienced leadership and coaching practitioners, people who understand group dynamics, business context, and what makes teams and leadership work. A simple activity becomes a live lesson in how people listen, solve problems together, and celebrate each other's wins.
